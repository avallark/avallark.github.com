Why a New Laptop?

Jun 10th, 2012

There are three reasons why I decided that I need to build a new laptop on my own. + There isn’t a good enough laptop I would use for my day to day use of browsing and programming. + There isn’t any laptop that is made for Linux or that comes with out of the box linux without me having to reconfigure everytime. + Linux is the best platform out there for programming.

Getting back to linux

I have been struggling recently to setup a decent development environment on my Macbook pro. Macbook pro is a fantastic machine. I could simply have wiped the OSX and then installed linux on it, but the keyboard layout, the general operating system in itself, all makes it a lot more difficult for us to get the basic programming needs satisfied. Especially if you have to make ready a test environment to run development off.

I spent over three hours last week trying to configure mysqldb, python, python-mysqldb on my mac. Now I have it configured on the mac, but now have to run python by calling python2.7. I didn’t want to change /usr/bin/python to point it to py2.7 because on a mac, you never really know what all you’d break if you play around with system settings.

All this on one of the later ubuntu machines is a matter of

apt-get install mysql-server mysql-server-dev python-mysqldb

This would have saved me over 2 days of work.

I have spent a LOT of time configuring my emacs, my postgres db and a ton of other applications simply to get it to work. Now that I have everything working, my Mac is constantly hot. This is simply because for the mac, all this is unnatural, the cocoa with osx is finding all these ports too much to handle. Time to do the development in the environment we think development should be made in, Linux.

Current laptops aren’t good enough

I have been looking around for a laptop that I can use to run linux on, and having used the Mac for the better part of the last two years, I can NOT find a laptop that even matches the standard of this machine. I simply love the keyboard, the monitor, the trackpad and the screen. The magnetic power connector is a wonderful example of the thought that has been put into developing this piece of hardware. Having been used to all this, its very disappointing to see the options that are available right now.

The only machines of quality available are the Dell XPS, the Sony VAIO and HP Envy, but all of them severely fall short of the expectations. In terms of power you have the Alienware who are great gaming machines, but are way too bulky for day to day use.

But who is going to use a laptop anymore?

There is very good reasoning behind the argument that everyone in the world is now moving towards tablets, and lighter and lighter laptops. I would very much argue, that just like we still have desktops in our offices, we will need good laptops for the years to come. There is a big hole in the market right now that nobody is innovating in the laptop market. Maybe laptops will change shape to become smaller, maybe the screens will be replaced by walls, maybe the they would become voice activated, but somebody needs to be spending money on innovating on this.

Of the 2.5 billion computing devices out there most of this world population wouldn’t be using the computer for computing puposes. My mother does not need a computer, she needs a device that can do skype and lets her access news. The era of special purpose computers is definitely arriving. With devices like the Raspberry pie these devices will storm the market.

One such special purpose device would be the computers that are to be used for programming needs. For this you would still need laptops/laptop like devices. For the forseeable future, with the technology that we have in the market right now, these devices would be very much laptop-like devices. In this age of the geek, where happy hacking keyboards (HHK) sell for 200 Euros a piece, a good quality laptop at price range of $3000 would not be too tough a sell.
